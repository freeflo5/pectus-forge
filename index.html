Pectus Stretching Protocol

Riley's daily routine to correct kyphotic posture, open the chest wall, and reduce rib flare. Do this every single day.

"Correcting the kyphotic spine and tight chest is an extremely important part in improving the pectus condition. Pull shoulders back, push chest out, keep back straight — in daily life, every moment." — Riley Byrne
01
Chest Wall Stretch
Stand sideways to a doorframe. Place your arm at 90° on the frame, elbow at shoulder height. Lean gently through the door to open your chest. Feel the stretch across your pec and front shoulder.
15 sec each side × 5 rounds
02
Prone Superman Stretch
Lie face down, arms extended overhead. Lift arms, chest, and legs off the floor simultaneously. Squeeze the glutes and rear delts. Hold at the top, lower slowly. Great for erector spinae and rear shoulder activation.
3 sec hold × 6 reps
03
Cobra Stretch
Lie face down, hands under shoulders. Press up, extending the spine, keeping hips grounded. Hold at the top. Excellent for opening the chest cavity and reducing the appearance of a sunken sternum.
15 sec hold × 3 reps
04
Arm Stretch Kneeling
Kneel on the floor with arms extended out onto a bench or chair at shoulder height. Slowly press your upper body down while keeping your back straight. Targets the chest and shoulder girdle.
15 sec hold × 3 reps
05
Reverse Crunch (Rib Flare)
Lie on your back. Drive knees to chest using abs only, curling the pelvis up. Think "ribs down" on every rep. This is critical for combating rib flare associated with pectus posture.
12 controlled reps × 3 sets
06
Shoulder Dislocation Stretch
Using a band or broomstick with wide grip, slowly rotate arms overhead and behind the back. Keep abs tight and ribs pressed down throughout. Key for correcting rounded shoulders.
10 reps × 5 sets
07
Side Plank with Reach Through
Hold a side plank. Reach top arm under your body (threading the needle), then rotate open reaching toward the ceiling. Works the obliques and helps correct the rotated pelvis posture from pectus.
30 reach-throughs each arm
08
Daily Posture Practice
Every waking hour: place a hand on your upper abdomen, exhale sharply through your mouth while crunching your abs together. Feel the ribs contract and depress. This is the single most important daily habit for pectus improvement.
As often as possible throughout the day

Rib Flare Protocol

1. Breathwork — Sharp exhale through the mouth at peak contraction on every exercise. The exhale automatically depresses the ribs.

2. "Ribs Down" Cue — Think this on every gym movement and in daily life. Exhale, crunch abs, feel ribs contract. Start right now in your chair.

3. Train Abs — The core exercises in every workout directly strengthen the muscles that control rib position. Never skip core work.

4. Posture Awareness — Standing, walking, sitting, lying. Shoulders back. Chest out. Back straight. Always.
