Recovery

Daily stretching & pectus protocol.

"Correcting the kyphotic spine and tight chest is an extremely important part in improving the pectus condition. Pull shoulders back, push chest out, keep back straight — always." — Riley Byrne
01
Chest Wall Stretch
Stand sideways to a doorframe, arm at 90°. Lean through the door to open your chest.
15 sec per side × 5 rounds
02
Prone Superman
Face down, lift arms, chest and legs simultaneously. Squeeze glutes and rear delts. Lower slowly.
3 sec hold × 6 reps
03
Cobra Stretch
Hands under shoulders, press up extending the spine. Hips stay grounded. Opens the chest cavity.
15 sec × 3 reps
04
Arm Stretch Kneeling
Kneel, arms stretched on a bench. Press upper body down, back straight.
15 sec × 3 reps
05
Reverse Crunch
Lie back, drive knees to chest using abs only. Think ribs down every rep. Critical for rib flare.
12 reps × 3 sets
06
Shoulder Dislocation
Band or broomstick, wide grip. Slowly rotate arms overhead and behind the back. Ribs down throughout.
10 reps × 5 sets
07
Side Plank Reach Through
Side plank, thread top arm under your body, then rotate open to ceiling. Corrects rotated pelvis.
30 each arm
08
Posture Habit
Every hour: exhale sharply, crunch abs, feel ribs depress. Do this all day, every day.
As often as possible
